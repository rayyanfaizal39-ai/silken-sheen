# Chapter 13: Meteoroid, Asteroid, Comet

## 13.1 Other Objects in the Solar System, such as Meteoroids, Asteroids and Comets

### Introduction

Besides the galaxy, stars and planets studied in Chapters 11 and 12, there are other objects present in our solar system: meteoroids, asteroids and comets.

### Important Concepts

* **Meteoroid:** A floating piece of stone and metal that moves in space.
* **Asteroid:** A large metal and rocky body that travels around the Sun in its own orbit.
* **Comet:** A small body made up of a mixture of ice, gas and frozen dust. It travels around the Sun in its own orbit.

* **Characteristics of a Meteoroid:**
  * Appears in various sizes, from 10 m to 1 m.
  * Made up of stones and metals such as iron and nickel.
  * Originates from fragments of asteroids and comets.
  * Its surface temperature in outer space is about 0°C.
  * Travels at varying speeds. The fastest meteoroid travels at 42 km s⁻¹.

* **Characteristics of an Asteroid:**
  * Size starts from 1 m to 1,000 km.
  * Made up of stones and metals such as iron and nickel.
  * Has a cold surface temperature, approximately −73°C.
  * Travels around the Sun at an average speed of 25 km s⁻¹.
  * Asteroids form an **asteroid belt** between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter.
  * Large asteroids such as Ceres, Pallas, Juno and Vesta have diameters of a few kilometres to 1,000 kilometres.
  * Asteroids are also known as small planets.

* **Characteristics of a Comet:**
  * Consists of two main parts: a head and a tail.
  * The length of the tail of a comet can reach up to 150,000,000 km. The size of the head can reach up to 250,000 km.
  * Comprises gas and water that freeze into ice, dust and rocky particles.
  * Travels around the Sun in its own elliptical orbit.
  * Travels at an average speed range of 10 km s⁻¹ to 70 km s⁻¹.

* **Movement of a Meteoroid:**
  1. A meteoroid is a small fragment of rock and metal that floats in outer space and travels around the Sun.
  2. When a meteoroid enters the Earth's atmosphere, it is called a **meteor**. Molecular friction between air and the meteor produces heat until it burns, resulting in a streak of light.
  3. **Meteor showers** happen when many meteors enter the Earth at the same time.
  4. Usually, meteors burn out before reaching the Earth. However, some meteors can reach the Earth — these are known as **meteorites**. Craters form as a result of meteorite impact.

* **Movement of an Asteroid:**
  1. Asteroids move at an average speed of 25 km s⁻¹. Most asteroids are in the asteroid belt; however, some asteroid orbits are outside the asteroid belt, such as the orbits of Apollo, Amor and Aten.
  2. Orbits outside the asteroid belt can be near or even intersect with the orbit of the Earth.
  3. Collisions can happen between asteroids and the Earth at the intersection point or trails near the orbits of the Earth and the asteroids.
  4. Collisions with large asteroids can result in the destruction of living things on the Earth.

* **Movement of a Comet:**
  1. A comet moves at a speed range of 10 to 70 km s⁻¹. Most comets are from the **Kuiper belt** and the **Oort cloud**.
  2. When a comet gets closer to the Sun, it increases in speed, melts and appears to have a long tail.
  3. The tail of a comet is always away from the Sun due to solar wind blowing from the Sun.
  4. Strong gravitational pull of outer planets causes the comet to easily slip out of its orbit.
  5. A comet that is out of its orbit has a risk of colliding with the Earth at high speed.

### Important Facts

* The Hoba Meteorite — the largest meteorite ever recorded in the world, believed to have fallen to Earth approximately 80,000 years ago, discovered near Grootfontein, Namibia, in 1920.
* A meteorite crater formed in Arizona, United States, approximately 50,000 years ago, with a diameter of approximately 1.2 km.
* Paleontologists believe the extinction of dinosaurs was caused by the collision of a 10 km sized asteroid with the surface of the Earth.
* The first direct collision of a comet with objects in the solar system occurred in 1994, between the Shoemaker-Levy 9 comet and Jupiter.
* The Halley Comet was last seen passing the Earth in 1986 and is expected to pass the Earth again in 2061.
* Scientists are always monitoring asteroids in space to ensure that asteroid orbits are at a safe distance from the orbit of the Earth. Warnings will be issued if there is a risk of collision; asteroids approaching the Earth may be destroyed or have their course changed.
* The National Space Agency (ANGKASA) is responsible for driving and monitoring the development of space science in Malaysia.

### Examples

* The asteroid belt is located between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter.
* The orbits of Apollo, Amor and Aten are examples of asteroid orbits outside the asteroid belt that can intersect with the Earth's orbit.
* The Halley Comet and the Shoemaker-Levy 9 comet are examples of well-known comets.

### Subtopic Summary

Meteoroids, asteroids and comets are other objects in the solar system besides galaxies, stars and planets. A meteoroid is a rock/metal fragment that burns up as a meteor when it enters the Earth's atmosphere (and becomes a meteorite if it reaches the Earth's surface). An asteroid is a large rocky/metal body, mostly located within the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. A comet is an ice/gas/dust body originating from the Kuiper belt and Oort cloud, with a tail that always points away from the Sun. Collisions of these objects with the Earth can have major effects, and scientists monitor asteroid orbits to avoid the risk of collision.
