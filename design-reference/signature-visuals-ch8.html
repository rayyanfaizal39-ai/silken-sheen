🌈 Dispersion Through a Prism

Actual spectrum order, actual bending amount per colour — red bends least, violet bends most.

Each colour travels at a different speed through glass — red is the fastest and bends the least, violet is the slowest and bends the most.

☀️ Why the Sky Changes Colour

Midday vs sunset scattering, shown as one continuous idea instead of two separate facts.

Sun is overhead — light takes a short path through the atmosphere. Blue scatters everywhere along the way, so wherever you look up, blue reaches your eyes → sky looks blue

Sun is at the horizon — light takes a much longer path through the atmosphere. Blue scatters away before it reaches you; only red/orange survive the trip → sky looks reddish

🎨 Adding & Subtracting Colour

Overlapping colour circles for addition, plus the object-reflection principle for subtraction.

Addition of light: Red+Blue=Magenta, Red+Green=Yellow, Blue+Green=Cyan, all three=White
White light hits the banana. It absorbs every colour except yellow — yellow is the only one reflected back to your eye, so that's the colour you see.
