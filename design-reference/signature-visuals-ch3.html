Signature Visuals — Chapter 3: Coordination and Response

Homeostasis is inherently a "dial swinging back to center" concept — so the visuals lean into that directly instead of describing it in prose.

🌡️ The Homeostatic Control Loop

A literal thermostat dial — the exact same mental model as an oven or air-con, applied to the body.

Brain detects the swing away from normal → triggers a corrective mechanism → needle returns to center. This one loop explains water regulation, temperature regulation, and every animal example in this chapter.

🧍 Hot Day vs Cold Day — Side by Side

Both responses shown at once, not as two separate paragraphs to compare mentally.

🔥 Hot Day

  • Blood vessels dilate (widen)
  • Hairs lie flat
  • Sweating increases
  • Body temperature decreases

❄️ Cold Day

  • Blood vessels constrict (narrow)
  • Hairs stand erect
  • Sweating decreases
  • Shivering — body temperature increases

🐕 How Different Animals Regulate

Five very different strategies for the same problem — a grid makes the variety obvious at a glance.

🐕
Cats & Dogs

Lick fur, hang tongue out to cool; fur stands erect when cold

🦎
Lizard (hot)

Heart beats faster, movement and metabolism speed up

🦎
Lizard (cold)

Body activity, movement and metabolism all slow down

🐌
Snail

Produces fluid, seeks humid places to reduce water loss

🐝
Bee

Closes spiracles between breaths to reduce water loss

🌿 Transpiration & Stoma Control

Water flow animated upward through the plant, and the stoma actually shown open vs closed.

☀️ During the Day — Stoma Open

Guard cells absorb water, become turgid (swollen), and bulge apart — opening the pore between them for gas exchange and transpiration

🔥 Temperature Too High — Stoma Closed

Guard cells lose water, become flaccid, and sit flush against each other — sealing the pore to reduce water loss
