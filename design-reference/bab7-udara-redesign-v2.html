Why this matters

Every breath you take is only ~21% oxygen — that's why astronauts, divers, and even candles all depend on getting that ratio exactly right. This chapter shows you how air actually works, why fires behave the way they do, and how pollution throws all of it off balance.

💡 Formal definition
Air is a mixture of gases that surrounds the Earth. Other components — water vapour, dust and microorganisms — are also present in small, variable amounts depending on place and time (e.g. more water vapour in forests or after rain).
🧪 What's inside "inert gases"?
Helium, Neon, Argon, Krypton and Xenon — five separate gases grouped together because they rarely react. Each has its own specific job later in this chapter.
🔬 Mixture or compound?
Air is a mixture, not a compound — because its gases can be separated by a physical method called fractional distillation (separating a liquid mixture into fractions with different boiling points, through boiling and condensation).

✅ Right — water rises to 1/5 of the gas jar. That's proof: oxygen was used up during combustion, and it makes up roughly 20% of air. Air is a mixture — you can tell because it's separable by physical methods, unlike a compound.

◆ Carbon & Oxygen Cycles

Keeping the Gas Balance

Two cycles working in opposite directions, constantly, to keep air's ratio stable.

🔵 Carbon Cycle

  • Green plants absorb CO₂ through photosynthesis
  • Animals eat plants and obtain carbon from them
  • Dead plants & animals decompose (bacteria/fungi), releasing CO₂
  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, petrol) releases CO₂
  • Respiration releases CO₂ — balanced again by photosynthesis

🟣 Oxygen Cycle

  • Respiration, rusting, combustion & decomposition all use O₂
  • Photosynthesis is the only process that releases O₂ back
  • This constant give-and-take keeps oxygen levels stable
🌍 Protecting the balance
🌳 Prohibit illegal logging
🌱 Reforestation
🚫 Don't burn forests
🧴 Avoid excessive pesticides
🚗 Prevent excess vehicle smoke

Combustion & Fire Safety

Combustion: a reaction when a substance is heated in the presence of oxygen, producing heat and light energy.

Cut off oxygen using a fire blanket, wet sack, mud, sand, soil or foam

Separate burning material, keep unburnt material away, cut off gas/oil supply

The Damage It Causes

Air pollution doesn't just affect breathing — it touches health, buildings, ecosystems and climate.

🫁 Health
  • Smoke & dust — breathing problems
  • Sulfur dioxide — respiratory problems
  • Carbon monoxide — headache, mental retardation, death
  • Asbestos particles — lung cancer
  • Lead particles — intellectual disability in children & babies
🏛️ Buildings & Infrastructure
  • Dust & soot stain buildings
  • Acid rain corrodes concrete & limestone
  • Acid rain speeds up iron rusting
🌱 Plants & Animals
  • Acid rain makes soil acidic & less fertile
  • Acid rain makes water acidic — unsuitable for aquatic life
  • Smoke & haze reduce sunlight — slows photosynthesis
🌍 Climate
  • Smoke from factories, vehicles & burning → haze
  • Excess CO₂ → greenhouse effect
  • Excess CFC → thinning ozone layer
  • SO₂ & NO₂ → acid rain
◆ 7.3 continued

Prevention & Control

Three fronts working together — rules, awareness, and technology.

⚖️ Law Enforcement
  • Fine smokers in restricted areas
  • Fine open burning
  • Fine vehicles emitting excess smoke
  • Prohibit factories in housing areas
🎓 Education
  • Teach students the effects & prevention of pollution
  • Anti-smoking campaigns
  • Encourage walking / cycling
  • Encourage public transport / carpooling
🔬 Science & Technology
  • Hybrid vehicle technology
  • HCFC instead of CFC in refrigerators
  • Install filters in factory chimneys
  • Catalytic converters on vehicles
  • Biological pest control instead of pesticides
